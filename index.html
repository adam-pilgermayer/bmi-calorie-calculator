Calculation formulas

BMI

Body Mass Index is used to calculate your obesity level.

BMI has 4 levels:

  • underweight (under 18.5)
  • normal weight (between 18.5 and 24.9)
  • overweight (between 25 and 29.9)
  • obese (above 30)

BMI Formula

STANDARD UNITS

Weight in lbs / (Height in inches)2 * 703

METRIC UNITS

Weight in kg / (Height in m)2

Calories

To calculate your calorie requirements, you need two formulas.

First, you have to calculate your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate). BMR shows your daily energy needs at rest.

Then, choose your PAL (Physical Activity Level). It has 5 levels.

BMR multiplied by your PAL level you'll get your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure), which is your daily calorie needs baased on your activity level.

BMR Formula

STANDARD UNITS

For Women: 4.536 * (weight in lbs) + 15.88 * (height in inches) - 5 * (age in years) - 161

For Men: 4.536 * (weight in lbs) + 15.88 * (height in inches) - 5 * (age in years) + 5

STANDARD UNITS

For Women: 10 * (weight in kg) + 6.25 * (height in cm) - 5 * (age in years) - 161

For Men: 10 * (weight in kg) + 6.25 * (height in cm) - 5 * (age in years) + 5

TDEE Formula

  • Sedentary (Little or no exercise) BMR * 1.2
  • Light (exercise 1-3 times a week) BMR * 1.375
  • Moderate (exercise 4-5 times a week) BMR * 1.55
  • Very active (6-7 exercise a week) BMR * 1.725
  • Extremely active (Physical job & hard exercise) BMR * 1.9
